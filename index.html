Steelwork

Steelwork - Stairs

Design Standards: Staircases shall conform to BS 5395 and the Building Regulations where appropriate.

Optimum Pitch: 38° with a going of 250mm and a rise of 190mm. The sum of the going and twice the riser should fall in the range 600-660mm.

Occasional Access: For less than monthly access, a pitch up to 45° can be used.

Steel Staircases: Designed in accordance with Wessex Water's Standard Drawings STD 860 (Steel Staircase & Handrails).

Handrails: Shall consist of a top rail 1100mm above the highest accessible surface, a mid-rail 500mm below the top rail, and a toe (kick) plate not less than 100mm high.

Ref: DS 220, Section 4; WIMES 9.02, Section 6.4.

Steelwork - Ladders

Vertical Ladders: Designed in accordance with Wessex Water's Standard Drawing STD 862 (Vertical Fixed Ladders).

Safety Cages: Always fitted to ladders used exclusively as an emergency escape route (e.g., from a digester where the main access is via a stair). For other ladders, a risk assessment is required.

Fall Arrest Systems: Required for ladders over 2000mm or where there is a risk of falling more than 2000mm.

Retractable Handhold: Required at the top of ladders for covered chambers.

Ref: DS 220, Section 4; WIMES 9.02, Section 6.6.

Steelwork - Handrails

General Requirements: Handrails shall be strong and rigid enough to prevent a person from falling.

Dimensions: 42 x 3.2 hollow sections (32 nominal bore).

Loading: General Duty as defined in BS 4592, with consideration for Heavy Duty loading where necessary.

Components: Top rail at 1100mm, mid-rail at 500mm below the top rail, and a toe plate not less than 100mm high.

Infill Panels: Required in specific circumstances (e.g., over water, around aeration tanks, etc.).

Ref: DS 220, Section 5; WIMES 9.02, Section 6.8.

Steelwork - Flooring & Walkways

Design Standards: Industrial flooring and walkways shall conform to BS 4592, BS EN 14122, and EEMUA 105.

Loading: General Duty loading as defined in BS 4592, with consideration for Heavy Duty loading where necessary.

Materials: Open-mesh type flooring, except in specific cases (e.g., around screenings plant, over potable water channels, etc.).

Removable Flooring: Individual panels shall be no heavier than 20kg for safe removal by one person.

Cut-outs: Required to permit removal without disturbing spindles, supporting brackets, or pipework.

Ref: DS 220, Section 3; WIMES 9.02, Section 6.7.

Steelwork - Guardrails

General Requirements: Guardrails shall be designed to withstand lateral loads of 360 N/m for gangways and walkways, and 740 N/m for platforms and landings.

Components: Handrails, knee-rails, stanchions, and toe plates.

Infill Panels: Required in specific circumstances (e.g., over water, around aeration tanks, etc.).

Access Openings: Self-closing gates are required for access openings, with the same design load as adjacent guardrails.

Ref: WIMES 9.02, Section 6.8.

Materials - General

Stainless Steel: Required for all materials in contact with potable water (Grade 1.4401).

Galvanized Steel: Blast cleaned to SA 2½ and galvanized to BS EN ISO 1461.

GRP: May be used for remedial repair work or in smaller sections of flooring. Must be non-slip and resistant to UV and chemical attack.

Ref: DS 220, Section 9; WIMES 9.02, Section 6.2.

Access Covers

Selection: Access covers shall have ratings suited for their application as determined by the Access Cover Grade Selection Chart in Appendix A of DS 220.

Hinged Assisted Lift: Required for heavier covers (>20kgf lifting effort) where regular access is required.

Locking Facility: All access covers that can be opened without tools shall incorporate a locking facility.

Safety Grills: Required for covers over chambers with a fall >2m, designed to withstand a 250kg load.

Ref: DS 220, Section 10.

Supporting Structures

Corrosion Protection: The level of corrosion protection to supporting structures shall be no worse than the surface covers.

Removable Support Beams: Must have a methodology of lifting in compliance with the Manual Handling Regulations.

Ref: DS 220, Section 11.

Testing & Compliance

Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT): Required for walkways, platforms, stairs, ladders, and guardrails.

Site Acceptance Testing (SAT): Visual inspection and load testing to confirm compliance with design standards.

Ref: WIMES 9.02, Section 7.

Pipework

1.0 Scope (Pipework-Specific)

Definition: Specifies requirements for non-buried pipework used in water and wastewater treatment, steam systems, hydraulic systems, and building services.

Applications:

  • Water Treatment: Process water, treated water, and process air (≤ 3 barg).
  • Wastewater Treatment: Raw sewage, sludge, and process air (≤ 3 barg).
  • Steam Systems: Steam and condensate for CHP/THP plants.
  • Hydraulic Systems: Lubrication and hydraulic oil systems.
  • Building Services: Pipework for kitchens, bathrooms, and central heating in treatment works.

Exclusions:

  • HVAC systems, odour control, biogas, chlorine, diesel fuel, and other specific applications.
  • Buried pipework (covered by CESWI).

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 1.0, Page 7.

2.0 General (Pipework-Specific)

Definitions:

  • Purchaser: The end user or their representative.
  • Supplier: Responsible for design, fabrication, installation, and testing.
  • Specified/Stated: Information provided by the Purchaser or Supplier.

Regulations & Standards:

  • Compliance with UK statutory regulations (e.g., Pressure Equipment Regulations, Pressure Systems Safety Regulations).
  • Adherence to BS EN 13480 (Metallic Industrial Pipework) and ASME B31.3 (Process Piping).

Quality Assurance: Suppliers must operate under BS EN ISO 9001 and BS EN ISO 14001 for quality and environmental management.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 2.1, Page 8; Section 2.2, Page 9; Section 2.2.4, Page 9.

3.0 External Operating Environment (Pipework-Specific)

Environmental Factors:

  • Location: Above ground, in chambers, or open trenches.
  • Temperature: Minimum and maximum ambient temperatures.
  • Corrosivity: Categories C1 (low) to Im2 (very high) as per BS EN ISO 9223.
  • Hazardous Areas: Classification for explosive atmospheres.

Special Hazards: High levels of pollutants, vibration from adjacent machinery, or other site-specific risks.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 3.0, Page 9; Section 3.2, Page 9.

4.0 Process Fluid Details & Operating Parameters (Pipework-Specific)

Fluid Details:

  • Type: Water, wastewater, sludge, steam, hydraulic oil, etc. (See Table 4: Primary Fluids Identification and Table 5: Auxiliary Fluids Identification).
  • Characteristics: Grit content, FOG (Fats, Oils, Greases), chemical dosing, and corrosive gases (e.g., H₂S).

Operating Parameters:

  • Pressures: Design Pressure (DP), Maximum Design Pressure (MDP).
  • Temperatures: Minimum, design, and maximum temperatures.
  • Flow Rates: Minimum, design, and maximum flow rates (See Table 2: Minimum and Maximum Flow Velocities for Pipework Conveying Liquids and Table 3: Maximum Velocities for Pipework Conveying Air).
  • Cyclic Variations: Consideration for cyclic pressure and temperature changes.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 4.1, Page 9-10; Section 4.2, Page 10.

5.0 Performance Requirements (Pipework-Specific)

Design Life:

  • Rigid Metallic & Plastic Pipework: 20 years.
  • Flexible Hoses: 10 years.

Maintenance: Pipework should be designed to be maintenance-free, but specific maintenance requirements must be communicated.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 5.1, Page 10; Section 6.1.8, Page 11.

6.0 Design Requirements (Pipework-Specific)

6.1 General

Material Selection: Based on fluid type, corrosion resistance, and compatibility. Avoid dissimilar metals to prevent galvanic corrosion.

Corrosion Protection:

  • Internal Linings: Cement mortar, epoxy, or FBE (Fusion Bonded Epoxy) for ductile iron and carbon steel.
  • External Coatings: Zinc, bitumen, or epoxy coatings as per WIMES 4.01.

Pressure & Temperature Ratings: Pipework must withstand specified pressure and temperature ranges, including surge pressures.

Cyclic Loading: Consideration for fatigue and stress, especially in steel pipework.

Flexibility: Accommodate thermal expansion, settlement, and vibration. Use of expansion joints, loops, and offsets.

Supports: Adequate to prevent sagging, vibration, and load transfer. Independent supports for machinery-connected pipework. Galvanized supports for carbon steel pipework to prevent corrosion.

Fittings:

  • Bends & Elbows: Long-radius bends preferred for liquids, especially in larger pipe sizes (DN ≥ 300).
  • Tapers: Gradual changes in diameter to avoid step changes.
  • Test Points: For pressure gauges and instruments, with isolating valves.
  • Purging, Flushing & Draining Points: Sized at 25% of the parent pipe size, with isolating valves.

Joining Methods:

  • Flanged Joints: PN 16 flanges, gasket selection (See Table 1: Gasket Selection Table), and bolt tightening (See Table B1, Table B2, and Table B3 for tightening torque details).
  • Spigot and Socket Joints: EPDM or nitrile rubber gaskets.
  • Threaded Joints: WRAS-approved thread tape or compounds.
  • Pressed Joints: Limited to non-toxic, non-flammable applications.
  • Mechanical Couplings: Sleeve and grooved types for flexibility and dismantling.

Flow Velocity:

  • Liquids: Minimum velocities to prevent sedimentation (See Table 2: Minimum and Maximum Flow Velocities for Pipework Conveying Liquids).
  • Air: Maximum velocities to prevent excessive noise (See Table 3: Maximum Velocities for Pipework Conveying Air).

Blockage Prevention:

  • Minimum pipe size of DN 80 for wastewater.
  • Rodding or jetting points for sludge, grit, and FOG applications.

Layout & Routing: Ensure accessibility for operation and maintenance. Avoid routing above doorways or along personnel access routes.

Air Release & Entry: Venting to release air during filling and draining. Automatic air valves at high points.

Purging, Flushing & Draining: Design for easy maintenance and cleaning. Dead legs in water pipework are prohibited.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 6.2.1, Page 12; Section 6.3, Page 12; Section 6.4, Page 12; Section 6.5, Page 12; Section 6.6, Page 12; Section 6.7, Page 13-14; Section 6.8, Page 15-16; Section 6.9, Page 17-24; Section 6.10, Page 28-29; Section 6.11, Page 30; Section 6.12, Page 30; Section 6.13, Page 31; Section 6.14, Page 31.

7.0 Design Requirements for Interfaces with Specific Equipment (Pipework-Specific)

7.1 Pumps

Inlet Pipework: Bell-mouth entrance to reduce friction losses. Short and straight to prevent swirl and vortex formation. Eccentric reducers for horizontal pipework to prevent air pockets.

Outlet Pipework: Isolating valves and check valves. Air relief valves to release trapped air.

Test Points: Pressure gauges at specific locations (e.g., between pump outlet and isolating valve).

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 7.1.2, Page 33; Section 7.1.3, Page 33-34; Section 7.1.4, Page 35.

7.2 Rotodynamic Pumps

Hydraulic Modeling: Required for complex systems.

Flooded Suction: Ensure pumps operate with a flooded suction to prevent cavitation.

Air Relief Valves: To release air during system filling.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 7.2.1, Page 36; Section 7.2.2, Page 36; Section 7.2.3, Page 37.

7.3 Positive Displacement Pumps

Flooded Suction: Ensure rapid and reliable priming.

Over-Pressure Protection: Pressure relief valves or bursting discs.

Sludge Cake Pumping: Stainless steel or FBE-coated carbon steel pipework. Minimum DN 80, with long-radius bends and full-bore valves.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 7.3.1, Page 38; Section 7.3.2, Page 38-39; Section 7.3.3, Page 40.

7.4 Blowers & Fans

Flexible Connections: To accommodate movement and vibration.

Noise Reduction: Design to prevent excessive noise from high-velocity air flow.

Condensate Drains: At low points to remove condensate.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 7.4.1, Page 40.

7.5 Flowmeters

Straight Pipe Lengths: Upstream and downstream as per manufacturer's instructions.

Isolating Valves: Not required unless specified by the Purchaser.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 7.5, Page 42.

8.0 Fabrication (Pipework-Specific)

8.1 Metallic Pipework

Bending & Forming: Hot or cold bending, with post-bend heat treatment if required. No cracks or buckles allowed.

Welding: Compliance with BS EN 13480-4 and ASME B31.3. Welding Procedure Specifications (WPS) and Welder Qualifications (WQT/WPQ). Stainless Steel: Purged with oxygen-free nitrogen or argon during welding. Post-Weld Treatment: Pickling of stainless steel welds to restore corrosion resistance.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 8.1.2, Page 43; Section 8.1.3, Page 43.

8.2 Plastic Pipework

Fusion Welding: Butt fusion for PE pipework, with bead removal for sewage and sludge applications.

Solvent Welding: For UPVC and ABS pipework, following manufacturer's instructions.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 8.2.1, Page 44; Section 8.2.2, Page 44.

9.0 Installation (Pipework-Specific)

9.1 General

Protection: Pipes must be protected from foreign material during installation.

Alignment: Pipe ends must align naturally without forcing.

Wall Penetrations: Use puddle pipes, wall-couplings, or seal rings.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 9.1, Page 44.

9.2 Flanged Joints

Alignment: Flange faces must align within 0.5% across any diameter.

Gaskets: Full-face gaskets preferred for raised-face flanges (See Table 1: Gasket Selection Table).

Bolt Tightening: Even tightening in diametrically opposite pairs, with multiple stages (30%, 60%, 100% of final torque) (See Table B1, Table B2, and Table B3 for tightening torque details).

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 9.2, Page 46; Section 6.9.2.4, Page 21.

9.3 Threaded Joints

Thread Tape: WRAS-approved for drinking water applications.

Galvanized Threads: Treated with cold galvanizing paint after installation.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 6.9.4, Page 22; Section 9.3, Page 47.

9.4 Mechanical Couplings

Alignment: Ensure proper alignment and setting-gap as per manufacturer's instructions.

Tying: Couplings must be tied to resist hydraulic thrust.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 9.4, Page 47; Section 6.9.6, Page 23.

10.0 Inspection & Testing (Pipework-Specific)

10.1 Inspection

Visual Checks: Ensure pipework dimensions, layout, and routing comply with drawings.

Weld Examination:

  • Visual: All welds.
  • Radiographic/Ultrasonic: 50% of butt welds for high-pressure pipework.
  • Magnetic Particle/Dye Penetrant: 50% of fillet welds for high-pressure pipework.

Non-Destructive Testing (NDT): As specified in the PARTICULAR SPECIFICATION.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 10.1, Page 48; Section 10.1.2, Page 49.

10.2 Pressure Testing

Hydrostatic Testing:

  • Test pressure calculated as STP = MDP + 5 bar or 1.5 x MDP (whichever is lower).
  • Minimum test duration: 1 hour, with no visible leakage.

Pneumatic Testing: For low-pressure air systems (≤ 3 barg). Test pressure applied in stages, with leak checks at each stage.

Reference: WIMES 8.08i1 - Section 10.2.2, Page 51; Section 10.2.3, Page 54.
